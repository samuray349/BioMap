Animal Spotlight

Snow Leopard

Panthera uncia

Vulnerable

Quick Facts

Family:

Felidae

Diet:

Carnivore

Habitat:

Mountain ranges

Lifespan:

15-18 years

Description

The snow leopard is a large cat native to the mountain ranges of Central and South Asia. Known for its beautiful, thick fur and elusive nature, it's perfectly adapted to cold, rugged environments. Its white-gray coat with black spots provides camouflage in rocky terrain.

5 Major Threats

  • Poaching for fur and bones (used in traditional medicine)
  • Habitat loss due to expanding human settlements
  • Climate change affecting mountain ecosystems
  • Decline in prey populations (wild sheep and goats)
  • Human-wildlife conflict (retaliation killing by herders)

Conservation Status

IUCN Red List

Vulnerable

Estimated population: 4,000-6,500 mature individuals remaining in the wild.

Did You Know?

Snow leopards can't roar! Unlike other big cats, they have different vocal cords and make sounds more similar to domestic cats. They can jump up to 50 feet (15 meters) in a single leap, which helps them navigate their rocky habitats with ease.
